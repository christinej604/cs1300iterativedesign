Iterative Design and Evaluation

Background

As a group of computer science students at Brown University, we wanted to iteratively design for a startup that is could be more practical in our daily life. We decided to design for a startup called Taro.

Sketching and Wireframing

To summarize above, Taro is a website designed to be a premium subscription/membership based platform for software engineers who want to future their career. The idea is that with a payment, Taro will give exclusive access to information about jobs and how to get a job ranging from Q&As with leads from top tech companies and creating optimized performance reviews. The idea is to focus on the networking and non-technical side of the job.

After looking at the concept of the startup, we assumed that our users consist of (1) software engineers currently employed, (2) undergraduate students looking for internship or post-grad opportunities, (3) recruiters at different companies. Software engineers who are currently employed would be impacted by this website because then they are able to learn tips and tricks about how to do their job better. Additionally, if they want to pivot companies, this website would provide valuable information for that. Undergraduate students would benefit from this because they are jumping into a field without much prior experience and would benefit from learning how to work as a software engineer and more specifically, how to work as a software engineer for a top tech company. Lastly, recruiters would benefit from the website because they are able to find software engineers who are ambitious and very much either currently on the job hunt or looking to further their career by switching companies. Our users who would interact with their desktop.

Mockups

From the wireframe, we made an interactive high-fidelity wireframe using Figma, and in our studio, we received feedback from the colleagues. Click here to interact with our hi-fi prototype.

Here are some critiques that we have received:

  • Hierarchy
  • Interaction
    • Add an interaction screen after clearing history.
    • Add an interaction that helps users sign out of their account.
  • Navigation
    • Current design is so that the user can click anywhere outside of "sign in" button to take you out of sign in box, but change it so that the user can click anywhere outside of the sign in box.
  • Learnability
    • Add more clarity to the filter titles in the expert advice section.
    • Add more clarity to what the salary page is about and its filter.
    • Add more clarity to how the "Events for You" and "Calendar" work on the "Networking Events" section.
  • Visual design
    • Change "lorem ipsum" to actual relevant words that would fit into the context.
    • Create consistency between expert advice and career advice.
    • Add a scroll bar to our pages to indicate that there are more content.
    • Fill in the white space with filter instead of creating a filter button.
    • Change 3 bars on the profile page to a button that expands all.
    • Include date, location, and time in the networking events along with a short blurb about the event.

We incorporated all the helpful critiques and finalized our prototype which can be found here.
